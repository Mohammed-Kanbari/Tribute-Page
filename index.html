Albert Einstein

The genius who changed the world


Albert Einstein, known for his playful personality and brilliant mind


Here's a timeline of Albert Einstein's life:


  • 1879 - Albert Einstein was born on March 14 in Ulm, Germany, to Hermann and Pauline Einstein.

  • 1896 - Einstein renounced his German citizenship and enrolled in the Swiss Federal Polytechnic School in Zurich.

  • 1905 - Einstein published four groundbreaking papers, including the theory of special relativity, introducing the famous equation E=mc². This year is known as his "Annus Mirabilis" or "miracle year."

  • 1915 - He completed the General Theory of Relativity, revolutionizing the understanding of gravity and the universe.

  • 1921 - Einstein received the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, a pivotal step in quantum theory.

  • 1933 - Due to the rise of the Nazis, Einstein emigrated to the United States, where he accepted a position at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.

  • 1939 - Einstein signed a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt, warning about the potential development of atomic weapons by Germany, which led to the Manhattan Project.

  • 1955 - Einstein died on April 18 in Princeton, leaving behind a legacy as one of the most influential scientists in history.

"Imagination is more important than knowledge. For knowledge is limited, whereas imagination embraces the entire world, stimulating progress, giving birth to evolution." —Albert Einstein
